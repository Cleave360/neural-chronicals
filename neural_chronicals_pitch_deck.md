## 02 — The Problem

The AI space moves faster than any human editorial team can track.

- **Information overload**: Thousands of AI papers, announcements, and products ship every week
- **Fragmented coverage**: Practitioners read 10+ newsletters and have no single authoritative digest

- **Signal vs. noise**: Business leaders, researchers, and builders need curation, not aggregation

The result: the most important AI story of the week is buried under noise.

## 06 — Technology & Infrastructure

Neural Chronicals is built on a modern, scalable web stack with zero operational overhead.

- **Frontend**: React 19, TypeScript 5.9, Vite 7, Tailwind CSS
- **Hosting**: Cloudflare Workers with global edge delivery (<50ms TTFB worldwide)
- **Performance**: SPA with immutable asset caching, `no-store` HTML for instant updates
- **Security**: HSTS, CSP, X-Frame-Options, full HTTPS with 301 redirect enforcement
- **AI Curation**: Kindred AI agent pipeline for content research, synthesis, and editorial review
- **Monorepo**: Template architecture supports rapid launch of additional titles (`app_2` ready)

The platform is **production-grade on Day 1** — not a prototype.

## 07 — Curation Model: How Kindred AI Works

Each issue follows a five-stage AI curation pipeline:

```
1. SIGNAL HARVEST    → Kindred agents scan papers, releases, and news across 200+ sources
2. RELEVANCE RANK    → Semantic scoring against each section's editorial criteria
3. SYNTHESIS DRAFT   → Long-form drafts generated per vertical, grounded in primary sources
4. EDITORIAL PASS    → Human-AI collaborative review; tone, accuracy, and voice alignment
5. PUBLISH & INDEX   → One-click deployment to Cloudflare edge, versioned in Git
```

This pipeline compresses a traditional monthly editorial workflow from **6 weeks → 6 days**.
